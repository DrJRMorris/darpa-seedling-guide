# DARPA Seedling Application Roadmap
## College of Science Faculty Guide

### What Are Seedlings?

DARPA seedlings are small, exploratory research projects designed to test high-risk concepts before committing to full programs. They aim to transform ideas from "disbelief" (clearly won't work) to "doubt" (might actually work)â€”which for DARPA is the sweet spot for new programs.

**Typical Parameters:**
- **Duration:** 3-9 months (up to 12 months possible)
- **Funding:** Less than $1M (often $300K-$500K)
- **Personnel:** Very limited team
- **Goal:** Answer a specific technical question or validate a novel concept

---

### Why Pursue a Seedling?

- **Speed:** Rolling submissions with potential 30-day awards for projects under $2M
- **Flexibility:** Open topics within broad technical areas
- **Access:** Direct connection to DARPA program managers and DoD community
- **Gateway:** Successful seedlings often lead to larger DARPA programs ($5M-$50M+)
- **Freedom:** Fundamental research with no publication restrictions

---

### Eligibility

- U.S. and non-U.S. institutions (universities, non-profits, small businesses)
- No citizenship requirements for fundamental research
- Single PI proposals (no co-PIs required, though team members allowed)
- FFRDCs, UARCs, and National Labs are excluded

---

### The Application Process

#### STEP 1: Identify the Right Office and Program Manager

Review DARPA's six technical offices and their current interests:

- **Defense Sciences Office (DSO):** Physical sciences, materials, quantum, mathematics
- **Information Innovation Office (I2O):** AI, cybersecurity, software, resilient systems
- **Microsystems Technology Office (MTO):** Photonics, quantum microsystems, semiconductors
- **Biological Technologies Office (BTO):** Biosecurity, human performance, bioelectronics
- **Tactical Technology Office (TTO):** Autonomous systems, platforms, manufacturing
- **Strategic Technology Office (STO):** Space, maritime, positioning/navigation

**Find program managers:** [https://www.darpa.mil/about-us/people](https://www.darpa.mil/about-us/people)

#### STEP 2: Contact the Program Manager

**CRITICAL:** Reach out to relevant PMs BEFORE submitting anything formal.

Email or call to:
- Describe your concept (2-3 paragraphs)
- Ask if it aligns with their interests
- Request guidance on framing for a proposal
- Discuss whether it's appropriate for a seedling

*PMs expect and welcome these conversations. They want to help shape good ideas.*

#### STEP 3: Submit Executive Summary (Strongly Recommended)

After PM discussion, submit a 2-page executive summary through the office-wide BAA.

**What to include:**
- Problem statement and why it matters for national security
- Your innovative approach
- What you'll accomplish in 6-9 months
- Required resources
- Your relevant expertise

**Outcome:** DARPA will respond with either:
- Encouragement to submit full proposal
- Feedback on how to improve the concept
- Statement of no interest (saving you time)

#### STEP 4: Submit Full Proposal

If encouraged, prepare full proposal using templates in the BAA.

**Key sections:**
- Technical approach (10-20 pages depending on office)
- Management plan
- Cost proposal with detailed budget justification
- Statements of work with specific milestones

**Timeline:** Allow 2-4 weeks for full proposal preparation after executive summary feedback.

### Tips for Success

1. **Think revolutionary, not incremental:** DARPA explicitly excludes evolutionary improvements
2. **Connect to national security:** Even basic science needs clear defense relevance
3. **Be specific about the seedling goal:** What specific question will you answer in 6-9 months?
4. **Leverage Virginia Tech strengths:** Highlight unique facilities, expertise, or capabilities
5. **Keep it simple:** Small team, focused objective, clear deliverables
6. **Budget realistically:** Include travel to Arlington for meetings with PM

---

### What Happens After Selection?

- Assigned DARPA PM becomes your mentor and project manager
- Regular check-ins and technical discussions
- Possible visits to DoD facilities
- If successful, potential invitation to propose full program
- Connection to DoD transition partners
